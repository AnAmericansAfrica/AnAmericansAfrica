### [Mau Mau Uprising, Imperial War Museums](https://www.iwm.org.uk/history/what-was-the-mau-mau-uprising)
This webpage provides a brief explanation of the Mau Mau Uprising, including their guerrilla warfare tactics, firearms, and the ongoing British detainment of Kenyans after the end of the war. Please note that some of the content and photographs on this page may be upsetting.
### [Mau Mau Detainee’s Letter, 1956](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/1030002625)
This letter from September 1956 was written by detainee Simon Peter Ngatia Macheria to the head office in charge of the Karatina Works Camp concerning his treatment by camp authorities. Please note that the content of the letter may be upsetting. [In 2013, British foreign secretary William Hague officially apologized on behalf of the government and agreed to compensate 5,228 Kenyans who were tortured during the war.](https://www.theguardian.com/news/2016/aug/18/uncovering-truth-british-empire-caroline-elkins-mau-mau)
